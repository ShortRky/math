The Derivative as a Rate of Change

Chapter 3: Derivatives Lesson 1 of 7 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Understand what a derivative represents and how it measures rate of change.

In one sentence: The derivative tells you how fast something is changing at a specific moment.

💡 The Idea

What is a Derivative?

The derivative of a function at a point is the instantaneous rate of change — how fast the function is changing right at that moment.

f'(x) = lim(h→0) [f(x+h) - f(x)]/h

Geometric Meaning

The derivative is the slope of the tangent line to the graph at that point.

Physical Meaning

Velocity

If s(t) is position, then s'(t) is velocity — how fast position changes

Why This Matters

Derivatives are everywhere — velocity, acceleration, growth rates, marginal cost, and more. They're the foundation of calculus.

📝 Example

Example: Find f'(x) for f(x) = x²

Step 1: Use the definition

f'(x) = lim(h→0) [(x+h)² - x²]/h

Step 2: Expand

= lim(h→0) [x² + 2xh + h² - x²]/h = lim(h→0) [2xh + h²]/h

Step 3: Simplify

= lim(h→0) (2x + h) = 2x

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Finding Derivatives from Definition

  1. Write the definition — f'(x) = lim(h→0) [f(x+h) - f(x)]/h
  2. Substitute — Plug in f(x+h) and f(x)
  3. Simplify — Expand and cancel terms
  4. Evaluate the limit — Let h → 0
  5. State the answer — f'(x) = [result]
