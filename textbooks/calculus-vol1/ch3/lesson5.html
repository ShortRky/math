The Product Rule

Chapter 3: Derivatives Lesson 5 of 7 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to differentiate products of functions.

In one sentence: The product rule says d/dx [f·g] = f'·g + f·g'.

💡 The Idea

The Product Rule

d/dx [f(x)·g(x)] = f'(x)·g(x) + f(x)·g'(x)

"First times derivative of second, plus second times derivative of first"

Why Not Just Multiply?

You can't just differentiate each factor and multiply. The product rule is necessary because both functions are changing.

Why This Matters

The product rule is essential for differentiating products of functions, which appear frequently in physics and engineering.

📝 Example

Example: Find d/dx [x²·sin(x)]

Step 1: Identify f and g

f(x) = x², g(x) = sin(x)

Step 2: Find derivatives

f'(x) = 2x, g'(x) = cos(x)

Step 3: Apply product rule

d/dx [x²·sin(x)] = 2x·sin(x) + x²·cos(x)

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Using the Product Rule

  1. Identify the two functions — What is f and g?
  2. Find their derivatives — f' and g'
  3. Apply the formula — f'·g + f·g'
  4. Simplify — Combine like terms if possible
