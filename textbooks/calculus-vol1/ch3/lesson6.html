The Quotient Rule

Chapter 3: Derivatives Lesson 6 of 7 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to differentiate quotients of functions.

In one sentence: The quotient rule says d/dx [f/g] = (f'·g - f·g')/g².

💡 The Idea

The Quotient Rule

d/dx [f(x)/g(x)] = [f'(x)·g(x) - f(x)·g'(x)]/[g(x)]²

"Low d-high minus high d-low, over low squared"

Common Mistake

You can't just differentiate numerator and denominator separately. The quotient rule is necessary.

Why This Matters

The quotient rule is essential for differentiating ratios of functions, which appear in many applications.

📝 Example

Example: Find d/dx [x²/(x+1)]

Step 1: Identify f and g

f(x) = x², g(x) = x+1

Step 2: Find derivatives

f'(x) = 2x, g'(x) = 1

Step 3: Apply quotient rule

d/dx [x²/(x+1)] = [2x·(x+1) - x²·1]/(x+1)² = [2x²+2x-x²]/(x+1)² = [x²+2x]/(x+1)²

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Using the Quotient Rule

  1. Identify numerator and denominator — What is f and g?
  2. Find their derivatives — f' and g'
  3. Apply the formula — (f'·g - f·g')/g²
  4. Simplify — Combine like terms
