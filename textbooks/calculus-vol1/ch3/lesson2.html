The Derivative as a Function

Chapter 3: Derivatives Lesson 2 of 7 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Understand that the derivative is itself a function and learn different notation.

In one sentence: The derivative of a function is another function that gives you the slope at every point.

💡 The Idea

Derivative Notation

There are several ways to write derivatives:

f'(x), dy/dx, y', Df(x)

All mean the same thing — the derivative of f with respect to x.

The Derivative Function

If f is a function, then f' is also a function. For each input x, f'(x) gives you the slope of f at x.

Why This Matters

Understanding that derivatives are functions helps us analyze how rates of change themselves change — which leads to second derivatives and beyond.

📝 Example

Example: If f(x) = x³, find f'(x) and f'(2)

Step 1: Find f'(x)

f'(x) = 3x²

Step 2: Evaluate at x = 2

f'(2) = 3(2²) = 12

Step 3: Interpret

The slope of f(x) = x³ at x = 2 is 12.

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Working with Derivative Functions

  1. Find the derivative — Use rules or definition
  2. Simplify — Write f'(x) in simplest form
  3. Evaluate if needed — Plug in specific x-values
  4. Interpret — What does the derivative tell you?
