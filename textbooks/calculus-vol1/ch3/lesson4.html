The Power Rule

Chapter 3: Derivatives Lesson 4 of 7 ⏱️ 25-35 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn the most important differentiation rule — the power rule.

In one sentence: The power rule says d/dx [xⁿ] = n·xⁿ⁻¹.

💡 The Idea

The Power Rule

d/dx [xⁿ] = n·xⁿ⁻¹

Bring the exponent down, then reduce the power by 1.

Examples

Basic Examples

d/dx [x³] = 3x²

d/dx [x⁵] = 5x⁴

d/dx [x] = 1

d/dx [1] = 0

Why This Matters

The power rule is used constantly in calculus. It's the foundation for differentiating polynomials and many other functions.

📝 Example

Example: Find d/dx [x⁷]

Step 1: Identify n

n = 7

Step 2: Apply power rule

d/dx [x⁷] = 7·x⁷⁻¹ = 7x⁶

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Using the Power Rule

  1. Identify the power — What is n?
  2. Bring n down — Multiply by n
  3. Reduce power by 1 — xⁿ becomes xⁿ⁻¹
  4. Simplify — Clean up the expression
