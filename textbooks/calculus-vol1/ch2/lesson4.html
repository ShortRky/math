One-Sided Limits

Chapter 2: Limits and Continuity Lesson 4 of 6 ⏱️ 25-35 minutes

🎯 Goal

Understand left-hand and right-hand limits and how they relate to two-sided limits.

In one sentence: One-sided limits look at what happens as you approach from only one direction.

💡 The Idea

Left-Hand and Right-Hand Limits

Left-Hand Limit

lim(x→a⁻) f(x) = L

"The limit as x approaches a from the left"

Right-Hand Limit

lim(x→a⁺) f(x) = L

"The limit as x approaches a from the right"

When Does a Two-Sided Limit Exist?

lim(x→a) f(x) exists if and only if:

lim(x→a⁻) f(x) = lim(x→a⁺) f(x)

Why This Matters

One-sided limits help us understand piecewise functions and functions with jumps or breaks.

📝 Example

Example: f(x) = {x if x < 2, x+1 if x ≥ 2}

Find lim(x→2) f(x)

Step 1: Left-hand limit

lim(x→2⁻) f(x) = lim(x→2⁻) x = 2

Step 2: Right-hand limit

lim(x→2⁺) f(x) = lim(x→2⁺) (x+1) = 3

Step 3: Compare

2 ≠ 3, so lim(x→2) f(x) does not exist

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Finding One-Sided Limits

  1. Identify which side — Are you approaching from left or right?
  2. Use the correct formula — Which piece of the function applies?
  3. Evaluate — Find the limit using that formula
  4. Check both sides — For two-sided limits, compare left and right
