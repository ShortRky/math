Limit Laws

Chapter 2: Limits and Continuity Lesson 3 of 6 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn the rules for calculating limits using algebra.

In one sentence: Limit laws let you break complex limits into simpler ones you can solve.

💡 The Idea

The Basic Limit Laws

If lim(x→a) f(x) = L and lim(x→a) g(x) = M, then:

Sum and Difference

lim(x→a) [f(x) ± g(x)] = L ± M

Product and Quotient

lim(x→a) [f(x) · g(x)] = L · M

lim(x→a) [f(x) / g(x)] = L / M (if M ≠ 0)

Power and Root

lim(x→a) [f(x)]ⁿ = Lⁿ

lim(x→a) ⁿ√f(x) = ⁿ√L

Why This Matters

Limit laws allow us to evaluate limits without making tables or graphs every time. They're the foundation for all limit calculations.

📝 Example

Example: Find lim(x→2) (3x² + 2x - 1)

Step 1: Apply limit laws

lim(x→2) (3x² + 2x - 1) = 3·lim(x→2) x² + 2·lim(x→2) x - lim(x→2) 1

Step 2: Evaluate each limit

= 3·(2²) + 2·(2) - 1 = 3·4 + 4 - 1 = 12 + 4 - 1 = 15

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Using Limit Laws

  1. Check if direct substitution works — Try plugging in first
  2. If it works — You're done!
  3. If not — Break into parts using limit laws
  4. Evaluate each part — Find limits of simpler pieces
  5. Combine — Use laws to get the final answer
