Limits at Infinity

Chapter 2: Limits and Continuity Lesson 6 of 6 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to find what happens to functions as x gets very large or very small.

In one sentence: Limits at infinity tell you the end behavior of a function — what happens as x goes to ∞ or -∞.

💡 The Idea

What Are Limits at Infinity?

lim(x→∞) f(x) = L means as x gets larger and larger, f(x) approaches L.

lim(x→-∞) f(x) = L means as x gets more and more negative, f(x) approaches L.

Horizontal Asymptotes

If lim(x→∞) f(x) = L or lim(x→-∞) f(x) = L, then y = L is a horizontal asymptote.

Common Patterns

Rational Functions

For f(x) = (axⁿ + ...)/(bxᵐ + ...):

  • If n < m: limit is 0
  • If n = m: limit is a/b
  • If n > m: limit is ±∞

Why This Matters

Limits at infinity help us understand long-term behavior — how populations grow, how costs change over time, and more.

📝 Example

Example: Find lim(x→∞) (3x² + 2x)/(x² - 1)

Step 1: Divide by highest power

lim(x→∞) (3x² + 2x)/(x² - 1) = lim(x→∞) (3 + 2/x)/(1 - 1/x²)

Step 2: Evaluate

= (3 + 0)/(1 - 0) = 3/1 = 3

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Finding Limits at Infinity

  1. Identify the type — Rational, exponential, etc.
  2. For rational functions — Divide by highest power
  3. Simplify — Cancel terms
  4. Evaluate — What's left as x → ∞?
  5. State the answer — The limit and any asymptote
