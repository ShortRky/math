Related Rates

Chapter 4: Applications of Derivatives Lesson 1 of 6 ⏱️ 35-45 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to find how fast one quantity changes when another related quantity is changing.

In one sentence: Related rates problems use derivatives to connect rates of change of different quantities.

💡 The Idea

What Are Related Rates?

When two or more quantities are related by an equation, their rates of change are also related.

If y depends on x, and both change with time t, then:

dy/dt = (dy/dx)·(dx/dt)

Common Scenarios

  • Expanding circles, spheres, or other shapes
  • Moving objects (cars, planes, people)
  • Filling or draining tanks
  • Shadow problems

Why This Matters

Related rates appear in physics, engineering, economics, and many other fields where quantities change together.

📝 Example

Example: A circle's radius increases at 2 cm/s. How fast is the area increasing when r = 5 cm?

Step 1: Identify variables

A = πr², dr/dt = 2 cm/s

Step 2: Differentiate with respect to time

dA/dt = 2πr·(dr/dt)

Step 3: Substitute known values

dA/dt = 2π(5)(2) = 20π cm²/s

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Solving Related Rates Problems

  1. Draw a diagram — Visualize the situation
  2. Label variables — What's changing?
  3. Write an equation — How are variables related?
  4. Differentiate — Take derivative with respect to time
  5. Substitute — Plug in known values
  6. Solve — Find the unknown rate
