Transformations

Chapter 1: Functions and Graphs Lesson 4 of 5 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to shift, stretch, and reflect graphs of functions.

In one sentence: Transformations change the position or shape of a graph without changing its basic structure.

💡 The Idea

Types of Transformations

Vertical Shifts

f(x) + k shifts the graph up by k units

f(x) - k shifts the graph down by k units

Horizontal Shifts

f(x - h) shifts the graph right by h units

f(x + h) shifts the graph left by h units

Vertical Stretch/Compression

a·f(x) stretches vertically if |a| > 1

a·f(x) compresses vertically if 0 < |a| < 1

Reflections

-f(x) reflects across the x-axis

f(-x) reflects across the y-axis

Why This Matters

Transformations help us understand how changing parameters affects a function's graph. This is essential for modeling real-world situations.

📝 Example

Example: Graph f(x) = (x - 2)² + 3

Step 1: Identify the base function

Base: g(x) = x²

Step 2: Identify transformations

Right 2 units: (x - 2)²

Up 3 units: (x - 2)² + 3

Step 3: Apply to key points

Vertex of x² is (0, 0)

After right 2: (2, 0)

After up 3: (2, 3)

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Applying Transformations

  1. Identify the base function — What's the original?
  2. List all transformations — Shifts, stretches, reflections
  3. Apply in order — Horizontal, then vertical
  4. Transform key points — Move important points
  5. Draw the new graph — Connect transformed points

✅ Check Yourself

Test Your Understanding

  • How does f(x) + 5 differ from f(x)?
  • What does f(x - 3) do to the graph?
  • How is 2f(x) different from f(x)?
  • What does -f(x) do?
