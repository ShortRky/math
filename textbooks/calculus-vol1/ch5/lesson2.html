Riemann Sums

Chapter 5: Integration Lesson 2 of 7 ⏱️ 35-45 minutes

🎯 Goal

Understand how to approximate areas under curves using rectangles.

In one sentence: Riemann sums approximate the area under a curve by adding up the areas of rectangles.

💡 The Idea

What Are Riemann Sums?

To find the area under f(x) from a to b:

  1. Divide [a, b] into n subintervals
  2. In each subinterval, pick a point
  3. Draw a rectangle with height f(x*)
  4. Add up all rectangle areas

Types of Riemann Sums

Left, Right, Midpoint

Left: use left endpoint of each subinterval

Right: use right endpoint

Midpoint: use middle point

Why This Matters

Riemann sums are the foundation of the definite integral. They show how integration is connected to area.

📝 Example

Example: Approximate ∫₀² x²dx with n = 4

Step 1: Find Δx

Δx = (b-a)/n = (2-0)/4 = 0.5

Step 2: Find points

Left endpoints: 0, 0.5, 1, 1.5

Step 3: Calculate sum

Sum = 0.5[f(0) + f(0.5) + f(1) + f(1.5)] = 0.5[0 + 0.25 + 1 + 2.25] = 1.75

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Computing Riemann Sums

  1. Find Δx — (b - a)/n
  2. Find points — Left, right, or midpoint
  3. Evaluate f at each point — Calculate heights
  4. Multiply and add — Sum of Δx·f(x*)
