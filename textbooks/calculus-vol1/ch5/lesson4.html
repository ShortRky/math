The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Chapter 5: Integration Lesson 4 of 7 ⏱️ 40-50 minutes

🎯 Goal

Understand the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, which connects derivatives and integrals.

In one sentence: The Fundamental Theorem shows that differentiation and integration are inverse operations.

💡 The Idea

The Fundamental Theorem

Part 1: If F is an antiderivative of f, then:

∫ₐᵇ f(x)dx = F(b) - F(a)

Part 2: If f is continuous, then:

d/dx [∫ₐˣ f(t)dt] = f(x)

What It Means

Part 1: You can evaluate definite integrals using antiderivatives

Part 2: The derivative of an integral gives back the original function

Why This Matters

The Fundamental Theorem is the most important theorem in calculus. It connects the two main operations and makes computation possible.

📝 Example

Example: Find d/dx [∫₀ˣ sin(t)dt]

Step 1: Apply Part 2

d/dx [∫₀ˣ sin(t)dt] = sin(x)

The derivative of the integral is the integrand!

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Using the Fundamental Theorem

  1. Identify which part — Are you evaluating or differentiating?
  2. For Part 1 — Find antiderivative, evaluate at limits
  3. For Part 2 — The derivative of ∫ₐˣ f(t)dt is f(x)
  4. Apply chain rule if needed — For composite functions
