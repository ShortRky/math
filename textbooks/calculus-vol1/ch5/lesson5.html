Integration by Substitution

Chapter 5: Integration Lesson 5 of 7 ⏱️ 35-45 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn the substitution method for evaluating integrals.

In one sentence: Substitution reverses the chain rule to simplify integrals.

💡 The Idea

What Is Substitution?

If you have ∫f(g(x))·g'(x)dx, let u = g(x), then du = g'(x)dx

∫f(g(x))·g'(x)dx = ∫f(u)du

When to Use It

Use substitution when you see:

  • A function and its derivative
  • A composite function
  • A complicated expression that can be simplified

Why This Matters

Substitution is one of the most powerful integration techniques. It's the reverse of the chain rule.

📝 Example

Example: Find ∫2x·cos(x²)dx

Step 1: Choose u

Let u = x², then du = 2xdx

Step 2: Substitute

∫2x·cos(x²)dx = ∫cos(u)du

Step 3: Integrate

= sin(u) + C = sin(x²) + C

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Integration by Substitution

  1. Choose u — Pick the inner function
  2. Find du — Differentiate u
  3. Substitute — Replace in the integral
  4. Integrate — Find the antiderivative
  5. Substitute back — Replace u with original
