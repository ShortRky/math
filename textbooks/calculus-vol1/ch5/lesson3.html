Definite Integrals

Chapter 5: Integration Lesson 3 of 7 ⏱️ 35-45 minutes

🎯 Goal

Understand what a definite integral is and how to evaluate it.

In one sentence: A definite integral gives you the exact area under a curve between two points.

💡 The Idea

What Is a Definite Integral?

The definite integral of f(x) from a to b is:

∫ₐᵇ f(x)dx = lim(n→∞) Σf(x*)Δx

It's the limit of Riemann sums as the number of rectangles goes to infinity.

Properties

  • ∫ₐᵇ f(x)dx = -∫ᵇₐ f(x)dx
  • ∫ₐᵃ f(x)dx = 0
  • ∫ₐᵇ [f(x) ± g(x)]dx = ∫ₐᵇ f(x)dx ± ∫ₐᵇ g(x)dx

Why This Matters

Definite integrals are used to find exact areas, volumes, and many other quantities in science and engineering.

📝 Example

Example: Evaluate ∫₀² x²dx

Step 1: Find antiderivative

∫x²dx = x³/3 + C

Step 2: Apply limits

∫₀² x²dx = [x³/3]₀² = (2³/3) - (0³/3) = 8/3

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Evaluating Definite Integrals

  1. Find the antiderivative — F(x) such that F'(x) = f(x)
  2. Evaluate at upper limit — F(b)
  3. Evaluate at lower limit — F(a)
  4. Subtract — F(b) - F(a)
