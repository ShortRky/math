Density of Rationals

Chapter 1: The Real Numbers Lesson 4 of 5 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Understand the density property of rational numbers and its implications for the real number line.

In one sentence: Between any two real numbers, there exists a rational number — the rationals are "dense" in the reals.

💡 The Idea

What Does Dense Mean?

A set is dense in ℝ if between any two real numbers, there's an element of that set.

For rationals: If a < b, then there exists q ∈ ℚ such that a < q < b.

Why Rationals Are Dense

Given a < b, we can always find a rational between them. Here's how:

Choose n large enough so that 1/n < b - a. Then find an integer m such that a < m/n < b.

This works because the rationals m/n get arbitrarily close to any real number.

Irrationals Are Also Dense

Between any two real numbers, there's also an irrational number.

Proof: If a < b, then a + (b-a)/√2 is irrational and lies between a and b.

Geometric Interpretation

On the number line, rationals and irrationals are "intertwined" — you can't draw a tiny interval without hitting both.

This is why the real line feels "continuous" — there are no gaps.

Why This Matters

Density explains why we can approximate any real number with rationals. It's essential for understanding limits, continuity, and why calculus works. It also shows that ℚ and ℝ are fundamentally different, even though ℚ is dense in ℝ.

📝 Example

Example 1: Finding a Rational Between Two Numbers

Problem: Find a rational number between 0.7 and 0.8

Step 1: Choose a denominator

We need 1/n < 0.8 - 0.7 = 0.1. So n = 10 works (1/10 = 0.1).

Step 2: Find the numerator

We need m/10 between 0.7 and 0.8. So 7 < m < 8. But there's no integer between 7 and 8!

Step 3: Try a larger denominator

Try n = 100. We need 1/100 < 0.1, which is true. Now we need 70 < m < 80. We can choose m = 75.

Step 4: Verify

75/100 = 0.75, and 0.7 < 0.75 < 0.8. ✓

Example 2: Density Doesn't Mean "All"

Problem: Show that √2 is not rational, even though rationals are dense.

Step 1: Assume √2 is rational

Suppose √2 = p/q where p, q are integers with no common factors.

Step 2: Square both sides

2 = p²/q², so p² = 2q².

Step 3: Analyze parity

p² is even, so p is even. Write p = 2k. Then 4k² = 2q², so q² = 2k², meaning q is also even.

Step 4: Contradiction

Both p and q are even, contradicting that they have no common factors. So √2 is irrational.

Key insight: Density doesn't mean every number is rational — just that rationals are "everywhere" on the number line.

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Using Density

  1. Identify the interval — What are a and b?
  2. Choose a denominator — Make 1/n < b - a
  3. Find the numerator — Find m such that a < m/n < b
  4. Verify your answer — Check that it's in the interval
  5. Remember the principle — Between any two reals, there's a rational
