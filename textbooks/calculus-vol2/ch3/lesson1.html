Parametric Curves

Chapter 3: Parametric Equations Lesson 1 of 5 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Understand how to describe curves using parametric equations and how to convert between parametric and Cartesian forms.

In one sentence: Parametric equations describe curves by expressing x and y as functions of a third variable (usually t).

💡 The Idea

What Are Parametric Equations?

Instead of writing y as a function of x, we write both x and y as functions of a parameter t:

x = f(t)
y = g(t)

As t varies, the point (x, y) traces out a curve.

Why Use Parametric Equations?

  • More curves: Some curves can't be written as y = f(x)
  • Direction: The parameter t shows the direction of motion
  • Multiple representations: The same curve can have different parametric forms
  • Physics: Natural for describing motion (position vs. time)

Converting to Cartesian Form

To eliminate the parameter:

  1. Solve one equation for t
  2. Substitute into the other equation
  3. Simplify

Why This Matters

Parametric equations are essential in physics for describing motion, in computer graphics for drawing curves, and in engineering for designing paths and trajectories.

📝 Example

Example 1: x = cos(t), y = sin(t), 0 ≤ t ≤ 2π

Step 1: Understand the curve

As t goes from 0 to 2π:

  • t = 0: (1, 0)
  • t = π/2: (0, 1)
  • t = π: (-1, 0)
  • t = 3π/2: (0, -1)
  • t = 2π: (1, 0)

Step 2: Convert to Cartesian

x² + y² = cos²(t) + sin²(t) = 1

Step 3: Conclusion

This is a circle of radius 1 centered at the origin, traced counterclockwise.

Example 2: x = t², y = t, -∞ < t < ∞

Step 1: Solve for t

From y = t, we get t = y

Step 2: Substitute

x = t² = y²

Step 3: Conclusion

This is a parabola opening to the right. Note: x is always ≥ 0.

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Converting Parametric to Cartesian

  1. Write both equations — x = f(t), y = g(t)
  2. Solve one for t — Choose the easier one
  3. Substitute — Replace t in the other equation
  4. Simplify — Get the equation in standard form
  5. Note restrictions — What values of x and y are possible?

Step-by-Step: Sketching a Parametric Curve

  1. Make a table — Calculate (x, y) for several values of t
  2. Plot the points — Mark them on the coordinate plane
  3. Connect smoothly — Draw the curve through the points
  4. Add arrows — Show the direction of increasing t
  5. Note the domain — What values of t are allowed?
