Power Series

Chapter 2: Sequences and Series Lesson 4 of 7 ⏱️ 35-45 minutes

🎯 Goal

Understand what a power series is, how to find its radius and interval of convergence, and how to work with power series.

In one sentence: A power series is an infinite polynomial that represents a function within its interval of convergence.

💡 The Idea

What is a Power Series?

A power series is an infinite series of the form:

∑(n=0 to ∞) aₙ(x-c)ⁿ = a₀ + a₁(x-c) + a₂(x-c)² + ...

Where c is the center and aₙ are the coefficients.

Radius of Convergence

Every power series has a radius of convergence R. The series converges when |x-c| < R.

Finding R

Use the Ratio Test:

R = 1/lim(n→∞) |aₙ₊₁/aₙ|

Or the Root Test:

R = 1/lim(n→∞) ⁿ√|aₙ|

Interval of Convergence

The interval of convergence is the set of all x-values for which the series converges.

Example

For ∑(n=0 to ∞) xⁿ, R = 1.

The interval of convergence is (-1, 1).

We must check the endpoints separately.

Common Power Series

Geometric Series

1/(1-x) = ∑(n=0 to ∞) xⁿ for |x| < 1

Exponential

eˣ = ∑(n=0 to ∞) xⁿ/n! for all x

Sine and Cosine

sin(x) = ∑(n=0 to ∞) (-1)ⁿx²ⁿ⁺¹/(2n+1)! for all x

cos(x) = ∑(n=0 to ∞) (-1)ⁿx²ⁿ/(2n)! for all x

Why This Matters

Power series allow us to represent functions as infinite polynomials. This is the foundation for Taylor series and many applications in physics and engineering.

📝 Example

Example 1: Finding the Radius of Convergence

Problem: Find the radius of convergence for ∑(n=0 to ∞) xⁿ/n!

Step 1: Apply the Ratio Test

|aₙ₊₁/aₙ| = |xⁿ⁺¹/(n+1)! · n!/xⁿ| = |x|/(n+1)

Step 2: Take the limit

lim(n→∞) |x|/(n+1) = 0

Step 3: Find R

Since the limit is 0 for all x, R = ∞.

The series converges for all x.

Example 2: Finding the Interval of Convergence

Problem: Find the interval of convergence for ∑(n=0 to ∞) (x-2)ⁿ/3ⁿ

Step 1: Apply the Ratio Test

|aₙ₊₁/aₙ| = |(x-2)ⁿ⁺¹/3ⁿ⁺¹ · 3ⁿ/(x-2)ⁿ| = |x-2|/3

Step 2: Find R

Converges when |x-2|/3 < 1, so |x-2| < 3.

R = 3, centered at c = 2.

Step 3: Check endpoints

At x = -1: ∑(-1)ⁿ diverges

At x = 5: ∑1ⁿ diverges

Step 4: Write the interval

Interval of convergence: (-1, 5)

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Finding the Radius of Convergence

  1. Identify aₙ — What are the coefficients?
  2. Apply the Ratio or Root Test — Find the limit
  3. Solve for R — Use the formula
  4. State your answer — R = [value]

Step-by-Step: Finding the Interval of Convergence

  1. Find R — Use the Ratio or Root Test
  2. Write the inequality — |x-c| < R
  3. Solve for x — Find the open interval
  4. Check endpoints — Test x = c-R and x = c+R
  5. Write the final interval — Include endpoints where it converges
