Introduction to Series

Chapter 2: Sequences and Series Lesson 2 of 7 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Understand what a series is, how to write it in sigma notation, and determine if it converges or diverges.

In one sentence: A series is the sum of the terms of a sequence, and we want to know if this sum approaches a finite value.

💡 The Idea

What is a Series?

A series is the sum of the terms of a sequence. If we have a sequence {aₙ}, the corresponding series is:

∑(n=1 to ∞) aₙ = a₁ + a₂ + a₃ + a₄ + ...

Example

Sequence: 1, 1/2, 1/4, 1/8, ...

Series: 1 + 1/2 + 1/4 + 1/8 + ...

In sigma notation: ∑(n=0 to ∞) (1/2)ⁿ

Partial Sums

We can't actually add infinitely many terms. Instead, we look at partial sums:

Sₙ = a₁ + a₂ + ... + aₙ

If the sequence of partial sums {Sₙ} converges, then the series converges.

Geometric Series

A geometric series has the form:

∑(n=0 to ∞) arⁿ = a + ar + ar² + ar³ + ...

Convergence

If |r| < 1, the series converges to:

S = a/(1-r)

If |r| ≥ 1, the series diverges.

Harmonic Series

The harmonic series is:

∑(n=1 to ∞) 1/n = 1 + 1/2 + 1/3 + 1/4 + ...

This series diverges even though the terms go to zero!

Why This Matters

Series are fundamental in calculus and appear everywhere in science and engineering. They're used to approximate functions, solve differential equations, and model real-world phenomena.

📝 Example

Example 1: Geometric Series

Problem: Find the sum of ∑(n=0 to ∞) (1/3)ⁿ

Step 1: Identify a and r

a = 1, r = 1/3

Step 2: Check convergence

|r| = 1/3 < 1, so the series converges.

Step 3: Find the sum

S = a/(1-r) = 1/(1-1/3) = 1/(2/3) = 3/2

Example 2: Writing in Sigma Notation

Problem: Write 2 + 4 + 6 + 8 + ... in sigma notation

Step 1: Find the pattern

The nth term is 2n.

Step 2: Write in sigma notation

∑(n=1 to ∞) 2n

Example 3: Finding Partial Sums

Problem: Find S₃ for ∑(n=1 to ∞) n²

Step 1: Write out the terms

S₃ = 1² + 2² + 3² = 1 + 4 + 9 = 14

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Working with Series

  1. Identify the type — Is it geometric, harmonic, or something else?
  2. Write in sigma notation — Express the series clearly
  3. Find partial sums — Calculate S₁, S₂, S₃, ...
  4. Check for convergence — Does the series approach a limit?
  5. Find the sum if it converges — Use the appropriate formula

Step-by-Step: Geometric Series

  1. Identify a and r — What are the first term and ratio?
  2. Check |r| — Is |r| < 1?
  3. Apply the formula — S = a/(1-r) if it converges
  4. State your answer — The sum or "diverges"
