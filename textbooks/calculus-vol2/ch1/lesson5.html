Improper Integrals

Chapter 1: Integration Techniques Lesson 5 of 6 ⏱️ 35-45 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to evaluate integrals with infinite limits or discontinuous integrands.

In one sentence: Improper integrals extend the concept of integration to functions with infinite limits or vertical asymptotes.

💡 The Idea

What Are Improper Integrals?

Improper integrals are integrals where either:

  • The interval of integration is infinite (like ∫₁^∞ f(x) dx)
  • The integrand has a vertical asymptote in the interval

Example: Infinite Limit

∫₁^∞ 1/x² dx

We can't evaluate this directly, so we use a limit:

∫₁^∞ 1/x² dx = lim(b→∞) ∫₁^b 1/x² dx

Types of Improper Integrals

Type 1: Infinite Limits

∫ₐ^∞ f(x) dx = lim(b→∞) ∫ₐ^b f(x) dx

∫₋∞^b f(x) dx = lim(a→-∞) ∫ₐ^b f(x) dx

∫₋∞^∞ f(x) dx = ∫₋∞^c f(x) dx + ∫c^∞ f(x) dx

Type 2: Discontinuous Integrands

If f has a vertical asymptote at x = c:

∫ₐ^b f(x) dx = lim(t→c⁻) ∫ₐ^t f(x) dx + lim(t→c⁺) ∫ₜ^b f(x) dx

Convergence and Divergence

An improper integral converges if the limit exists and is finite.

An improper integral diverges if the limit is infinite or doesn't exist.

Example

∫₁^∞ 1/x² dx converges to 1

∫₁^∞ 1/x dx diverges

Why This Matters

Improper integrals are essential for probability, physics, and engineering. They allow us to calculate areas and volumes that would otherwise be impossible to compute.

📝 Example

Example 1: Infinite Upper Limit

Problem: Evaluate ∫₁^∞ 1/x² dx

Step 1: Write as a limit

∫₁^∞ 1/x² dx = lim(b→∞) ∫₁^b 1/x² dx

Step 2: Evaluate the definite integral

∫₁^b 1/x² dx = [-1/x]₁^b = -1/b - (-1/1) = 1 - 1/b

Step 3: Take the limit

lim(b→∞) (1 - 1/b) = 1 - 0 = 1

Step 4: Conclusion

The integral converges to 1.

Example 2: Infinite Lower Limit

Problem: Evaluate ∫₋∞^0 eˣ dx

Step 1: Write as a limit

∫₋∞^0 eˣ dx = lim(a→-∞) ∫ₐ^0 eˣ dx

Step 2: Evaluate the definite integral

∫ₐ^0 eˣ dx = [eˣ]ₐ^0 = e⁰ - eᵃ = 1 - eᵃ

Step 3: Take the limit

lim(a→-∞) (1 - eᵃ) = 1 - 0 = 1

Step 4: Conclusion

The integral converges to 1.

Example 3: Discontinuous Integrand

Problem: Evaluate ∫₀¹ 1/√x dx

Step 1: Identify the problem

The integrand 1/√x has a vertical asymptote at x = 0.

Step 2: Write as a limit

∫₀¹ 1/√x dx = lim(a→0⁺) ∫ₐ¹ 1/√x dx

Step 3: Evaluate the definite integral

∫ₐ¹ 1/√x dx = [2√x]ₐ¹ = 2√1 - 2√a = 2 - 2√a

Step 4: Take the limit

lim(a→0⁺) (2 - 2√a) = 2 - 0 = 2

Step 5: Conclusion

The integral converges to 2.

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Evaluating Improper Integrals

  1. Identify the type — Infinite limit or discontinuity?
  2. Write as a limit — Replace infinity or problematic point with a variable
  3. Evaluate the definite integral — Use standard integration
  4. Take the limit — Evaluate as the variable approaches its limit
  5. State convergence — Does it converge or diverge?

Step-by-Step: Checking Convergence

  1. Set up the limit — Write the improper integral as a limit
  2. Evaluate — Find the antiderivative
  3. Take the limit — See if it exists and is finite
  4. Conclude — State whether it converges or diverges
