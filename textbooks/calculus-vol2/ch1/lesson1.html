Integration by Parts

Chapter 1: Integration Techniques Lesson 1 of 6 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn the integration by parts technique to solve integrals that are products of two functions.

In one sentence: Integration by parts is the reverse of the product rule for derivatives.

💡 The Idea

Where Does It Come From?

Integration by parts comes from the product rule for derivatives. Remember:

d/dx[u·v] = u·dv/dx + v·du/dx

If we integrate both sides and rearrange, we get:

∫u·dv = u·v - ∫v·du

This is the integration by parts formula!

The Formula

∫u·dv = u·v - ∫v·du

Where:

  • u = a function you choose to differentiate
  • dv = a function you choose to integrate
  • du = the derivative of u
  • v = the integral of dv

How to Choose u and dv

Use the LIATE rule to choose u (pick the first one that appears):

  • L - Logarithmic functions (ln x, log x)
  • I - Inverse trig functions (arcsin, arctan)
  • A - Algebraic functions (x², x³)
  • T - Trigonometric functions (sin x, cos x)
  • E - Exponential functions (eˣ, 2ˣ)

Why This Matters

Many integrals can't be solved with basic rules. Integration by parts gives us a powerful tool to tackle products of functions, which appear frequently in physics, engineering, and economics.

📝 Example

Example 1: ∫x·eˣ dx

Step 1: Choose u and dv

Using LIATE: x is Algebraic, eˣ is Exponential

So: u = x, dv = eˣ dx

Step 2: Find du and v

du = dx
v = ∫eˣ dx = eˣ

Step 3: Apply the formula

∫x·eˣ dx = u·v - ∫v·du
= x·eˣ - ∫eˣ dx
= x·eˣ - eˣ + C
= eˣ(x - 1) + C

Example 2: ∫ln(x) dx

Step 1: Choose u and dv

This is just ln(x) · 1, so:

u = ln(x), dv = dx

Step 2: Find du and v

du = (1/x) dx
v = ∫dx = x

Step 3: Apply the formula

∫ln(x) dx = u·v - ∫v·du
= ln(x)·x - ∫x·(1/x) dx
= x·ln(x) - ∫dx
= x·ln(x) - x + C

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Integration by Parts

  1. Identify the integral — Is it a product of two functions?
  2. Choose u and dv — Use LIATE rule for u
  3. Find du — Differentiate u
  4. Find v — Integrate dv
  5. Apply the formula — ∫u·dv = u·v - ∫v·du
  6. Simplify — Evaluate the new integral
  7. Add +C — Don't forget the constant of integration

Step-by-Step: When to Use Integration by Parts Again

  1. Check the new integral — Is it still a product?
  2. If yes — Apply integration by parts again
  3. If you get the original integral back — Solve for it algebraically
  4. Keep going — Until you get an integral you can solve
