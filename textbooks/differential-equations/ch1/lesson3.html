Linear Equations

Chapter 1: First-Order Differential Equations Lesson 3 of 6 ⏱️ 35-45 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to solve first-order linear differential equations using integrating factors.

In one sentence: Linear equations can be solved by multiplying by an integrating factor that makes the left side a perfect derivative.

💡 The Idea

Standard Form

A first-order linear equation has the form:

dy/dx + P(x)y = Q(x)

Where P(x) and Q(x) are functions of x only.

The Integrating Factor

The integrating factor is:

μ(x) = e^(∫P(x)dx)

Multiplying both sides by μ(x) makes the left side a perfect derivative:

d/dx[μ(x)y] = μ(x)Q(x)

Solution

The general solution is:

y = (1/μ(x)) ∫μ(x)Q(x)dx + C/μ(x)

Why This Matters

Linear equations appear everywhere in science and engineering — from population growth to electrical circuits. The integrating factor method is a powerful, systematic approach.

📝 Example

Example 1: Solve dy/dx + 2y = 4

Step 1: Identify P(x) and Q(x)

P(x) = 2, Q(x) = 4

Step 2: Find the integrating factor

μ(x) = e^(∫2dx) = e^(2x)

Step 3: Multiply both sides by μ(x)

e^(2x)dy/dx + 2e^(2x)y = 4e^(2x)
d/dx[e^(2x)y] = 4e^(2x)

Step 4: Integrate both sides

e^(2x)y = ∫4e^(2x)dx = 2e^(2x) + C

Step 5: Solve for y

y = 2 + Ce^(-2x)

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Solving Linear Equations

  1. Put in standard form — dy/dx + P(x)y = Q(x)
  2. Find P(x) — The coefficient of y
  3. Compute integrating factor — μ(x) = e^(∫P(x)dx)
  4. Multiply both sides — By μ(x)
  5. Integrate both sides — The left side becomes d/dx[μ(x)y]
  6. Solve for y — Divide by μ(x)
