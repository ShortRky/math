Stability Analysis

Chapter 3: Systems of Differential Equations Lesson 5 of 5 ⏱️ 40-50 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to determine the stability of equilibrium points in systems of differential equations.

In one sentence: Stability tells you whether small perturbations grow or decay — essential for understanding real-world systems.

💡 The Idea

What is Stability?

An equilibrium point is:

  • Stable: Solutions that start nearby stay nearby
  • Asymptotically stable: Solutions that start nearby approach the equilibrium
  • Unstable: Solutions that start nearby move away

Determining Stability from Eigenvalues

For a linear system dX/dt = AX:

  • All eigenvalues have negative real parts: Asymptotically stable
  • Any eigenvalue has positive real part: Unstable
  • Eigenvalues have zero real parts: Marginal (need more analysis)

Summary Table

Stability Classification

Stable node: Both eigenvalues real and negative

Unstable node: Both eigenvalues real and positive

Stable spiral: Complex eigenvalues, negative real part

Unstable spiral: Complex eigenvalues, positive real part

Saddle: Real eigenvalues with opposite signs (unstable)

Center: Purely imaginary eigenvalues (marginally stable)

Why Stability Matters

  • Engineering: Will the bridge collapse? Will the circuit oscillate?
  • Biology: Will the population survive? Will the disease spread?
  • Economics: Will the market stabilize? Will inflation grow?

Why This Matters

Stability analysis is crucial for predicting long-term behavior. It tells you whether a system will return to equilibrium after a disturbance — essential for safety and design in engineering and science.

📝 Example

Example 1: Determine stability

Problem: Determine the stability of the origin for dX/dt = [[-3, 1], [1, -3]]X

Step 1: Find eigenvalues

det(A - λI) = (-3-λ)² - 1 = 0
λ² + 6λ + 8 = 0
(λ + 2)(λ + 4) = 0
λ₁ = -2, λ₂ = -4

Step 2: Determine stability

Both eigenvalues are real and negative → Asymptotically stable

Step 3: Interpret

All solutions approach the origin as t → ∞. The system is stable.

Example 2: Unstable system

Problem: Determine the stability of the origin for dX/dt = [[1, 2], [2, 1]]X

Step 1: Find eigenvalues

det(A - λI) = (1-λ)² - 4 = 0
λ² - 2λ - 3 = 0
(λ - 3)(λ + 1) = 0
λ₁ = 3, λ₂ = -1

Step 2: Determine stability

One eigenvalue is positive → Unstable (saddle point)

Step 3: Interpret

Solutions move away from the origin along the direction of the positive eigenvalue.

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Stability Analysis

  1. Find eigenvalues — Solve det(A - λI) = 0
  2. Check real parts — Are they positive, negative, or zero?
  3. Apply the rule — All negative = stable, any positive = unstable
  4. Determine the type — Node, spiral, saddle, or center?
  5. Interpret — What does this mean for the system?
