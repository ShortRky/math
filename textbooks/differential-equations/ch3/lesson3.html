Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

Chapter 3: Systems of Differential Equations Lesson 3 of 5 ⏱️ 40-50 minutes

🎯 Goal

Master finding eigenvalues and eigenvectors, which are essential for solving systems of differential equations.

In one sentence: Eigenvalues and eigenvectors reveal the fundamental behavior of a system — how it grows, decays, or oscillates.

💡 The Idea

Definitions

For a matrix A:

  • Eigenvalue λ: A scalar where Av = λv for some nonzero vector v
  • Eigenvector v: A nonzero vector where Av = λv

Finding Eigenvalues

Solve the characteristic equation:

det(A - λI) = 0

This gives a polynomial in λ. The roots are the eigenvalues.

Finding Eigenvectors

For each eigenvalue λ, solve:

(A - λI)v = 0

The solutions are the eigenvectors for that eigenvalue.

Types of Eigenvalues

  • Real distinct: Different real numbers
  • Repeated: Same eigenvalue appears multiple times
  • Complex: Come in conjugate pairs a ± bi

Why This Matters

Eigenvalues and eigenvectors are the key to understanding systems. They determine stability, oscillation frequency, and long-term behavior. They're used throughout science and engineering.

📝 Example

Example 1: Find eigenvalues and eigenvectors

Problem: Find eigenvalues and eigenvectors of A = [[4, 1], [2, 3]]

Step 1: Find eigenvalues

det(A - λI) = (4-λ)(3-λ) - 2 = 0
λ² - 7λ + 10 = 0
(λ - 2)(λ - 5) = 0
λ₁ = 2, λ₂ = 5

Step 2: Find eigenvector for λ₁ = 2

(A - 2I)v = 0
[[2, 1], [2, 1]]v = 0
2v₁ + v₂ = 0 → v₂ = -2v₁
v₁ = [1, -2]ᵀ

Step 3: Find eigenvector for λ₂ = 5

(A - 5I)v = 0
[[-1, 1], [2, -2]]v = 0
-v₁ + v₂ = 0 → v₂ = v₁
v₂ = [1, 1]ᵀ

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Finding Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

  1. Write A - λI — Subtract λ from diagonal
  2. Compute determinant — det(A - λI)
  3. Solve for λ — Find roots of the polynomial
  4. For each λ, solve (A - λI)v = 0 — Find eigenvectors
  5. Check your work — Verify Av = λv
