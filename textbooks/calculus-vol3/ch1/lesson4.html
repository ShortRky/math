The Cross Product

Chapter 1: Vectors and the Geometry of Space Lesson 4 of 6 ⏱️ 35-45 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to compute the cross product of two vectors and understand its geometric meaning.

In one sentence: The cross product creates a new vector that is perpendicular to both original vectors.

💡 The Idea

What is the Cross Product?

The cross product of two vectors is a vector that is perpendicular to both original vectors.

u × v = ⟨u₂v₃ - u₃v₂, u₃v₁ - u₁v₃, u₁v₂ - u₂v₁⟩

Or using the determinant:

u × v = |i j k | |u₁ u₂ u₃| |v₁ v₂ v₃|

Properties of the Cross Product

  • Anti-commutative: u × v = -(v × u)
  • Not associative: (u × v) × w ≠ u × (v × w)
  • Distributive: u × (v + w) = u × v + u × w
  • Parallel vectors: u × v = 0 if and only if u ∥ v

Geometric Meaning

The magnitude of the cross product is:

|u × v| = |u||v|sin(θ)

This equals the area of the parallelogram formed by u and v.

Right-Hand Rule

The direction of u × v follows the right-hand rule:

  1. Point your fingers in the direction of u
  2. Curl them toward v
  3. Your thumb points in the direction of u × v

Why This Matters

The cross product is essential for finding normal vectors, calculating torque, determining if vectors are parallel, and many applications in physics and engineering.

📝 Example

Example 1: Computing the Cross Product

Problem: Find u × v for u = ⟨1, 2, 3⟩ and v = ⟨4, 5, 6⟩

Step 1: Set up the determinant

u × v = |i j k | |1 2 3| |4 5 6|

Step 2: Expand the determinant

= i(2·6 - 3·5) - j(1·6 - 3·4) + k(1·5 - 2·4)
= i(12 - 15) - j(6 - 12) + k(5 - 8)
= -3i + 6j - 3k

Step 3: Write in component form

u × v = ⟨-3, 6, -3⟩

Example 2: Finding the Area of a Parallelogram

Problem: Find the area of the parallelogram formed by u = ⟨1, 0, 0⟩ and v = ⟨0, 1, 0⟩

Step 1: Find u × v

u × v = |i j k | |1 0 0| |0 1 0|
= i(0 - 0) - j(0 - 0) + k(1 - 0)
= ⟨0, 0, 1⟩

Step 2: Find the magnitude

|u × v| = √(0² + 0² + 1²) = 1

Step 3: Interpret

The area of the parallelogram is 1 square unit.

Example 3: Finding a Normal Vector

Problem: Find a vector perpendicular to both u = ⟨1, 2, 3⟩ and v = ⟨4, 5, 6⟩

Step 1: Compute u × v

u × v = ⟨-3, 6, -3⟩

Step 2: Verify perpendicularity

(u × v) · u = (-3)(1) + (6)(2) + (-3)(3) = -3 + 12 - 9 = 0 ✓
(u × v) · v = (-3)(4) + (6)(5) + (-3)(6) = -12 + 30 - 18 = 0 ✓

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Computing the Cross Product

  1. Set up the determinant — Write i, j, k in the first row
  2. Expand the determinant — Use cofactor expansion
  3. Simplify — Combine like terms
  4. Write in component form — ⟨result₁, result₂, result₃⟩

Step-by-Step: Finding Area of Parallelogram

  1. Find u × v — Compute the cross product
  2. Find |u × v| — Compute the magnitude
  3. Interpret — The magnitude is the area
