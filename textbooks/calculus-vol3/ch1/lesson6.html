Cylindrical and Spherical Coordinates

Chapter 1: Vectors and the Geometry of Space Lesson 6 of 6 ⏱️ 40-50 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to use cylindrical and spherical coordinates to describe points in three-dimensional space.

In one sentence: Cylindrical and spherical coordinates provide alternative ways to describe 3D space that are often more convenient than Cartesian coordinates.

💡 The Idea

Cylindrical Coordinates

Cylindrical coordinates use (r, θ, z) where:

  • r = distance from the z-axis
  • θ = angle in the xy-plane from the positive x-axis
  • z = height (same as Cartesian z)

Conversion Formulas

From cylindrical to Cartesian:

x = r cos(θ)
y = r sin(θ)
z = z

From Cartesian to cylindrical:

r = √(x² + y²)
θ = arctan(y/x)
z = z

Spherical Coordinates

Spherical coordinates use (ρ, θ, φ) where:

  • ρ = distance from the origin
  • θ = angle in the xy-plane from the positive x-axis
  • φ = angle from the positive z-axis

Conversion Formulas

From spherical to Cartesian:

x = ρ sin(φ) cos(θ)
y = ρ sin(φ) sin(θ)
z = ρ cos(φ)

From Cartesian to spherical:

ρ = √(x² + y² + z²)
θ = arctan(y/x)
φ = arccos(z/ρ)

When to Use Each System

  • Cartesian: General purpose, rectangular shapes
  • Cylindrical: Cylindrical symmetry, problems involving cylinders
  • Spherical: Spherical symmetry, problems involving spheres

Why This Matters

Different coordinate systems make different problems easier. Choosing the right system can simplify calculations significantly in physics, engineering, and mathematics.

📝 Example

Example 1: Converting from Cartesian to Cylindrical

Problem: Convert (3, 4, 5) to cylindrical coordinates

Step 1: Find r

r = √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5

Step 2: Find θ

θ = arctan(4/3) ≈ 53.13°

Step 3: Keep z

z = 5

Step 4: Write the answer

(r, θ, z) = (5, 53.13°, 5)

Example 2: Converting from Cartesian to Spherical

Problem: Convert (1, 1, √2) to spherical coordinates

Step 1: Find ρ

ρ = √(1² + 1² + (√2)²) = √(1 + 1 + 2) = √4 = 2

Step 2: Find θ

θ = arctan(1/1) = 45°

Step 3: Find φ

φ = arccos(√2/2) = 45°

Step 4: Write the answer

(ρ, θ, φ) = (2, 45°, 45°)

Example 3: Converting from Spherical to Cartesian

Problem: Convert (4, 60°, 30°) to Cartesian coordinates

Step 1: Find x

x = 4 sin(30°) cos(60°) = 4 · 0.5 · 0.5 = 1

Step 2: Find y

y = 4 sin(30°) sin(60°) = 4 · 0.5 · (√3/2) = √3

Step 3: Find z

z = 4 cos(30°) = 4 · (√3/2) = 2√3

Step 4: Write the answer

(x, y, z) = (1, √3, 2√3)

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Converting Between Coordinate Systems

  1. Identify the systems — What are you converting from and to?
  2. Use the conversion formulas — Apply the appropriate formulas
  3. Calculate each coordinate — Find r, θ, z or ρ, θ, φ
  4. Write the answer — Use the correct notation

Step-by-Step: Choosing a Coordinate System

  1. Look for symmetry — Is there cylindrical or spherical symmetry?
  2. Consider the problem — What are you trying to find?
  3. Choose the simplest — Which system makes the math easiest?
  4. Convert if needed — You can always convert back to Cartesian
