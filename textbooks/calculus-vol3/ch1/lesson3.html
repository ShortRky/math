The Dot Product

Chapter 1: Vectors and the Geometry of Space Lesson 3 of 6 ⏱️ 35-45 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to compute the dot product of two vectors and understand its geometric meaning.

In one sentence: The dot product measures how much two vectors point in the same direction.

💡 The Idea

What is the Dot Product?

The dot product of two vectors is a scalar (a number) that measures their alignment.

u · v = u₁v₁ + u₂v₂ + u₃v₃

Or equivalently:

u · v = |u||v|cos(θ)

Where θ is the angle between the vectors.

Properties of the Dot Product

  • Commutative: u · v = v · u
  • Distributive: u · (v + w) = u · v + u · w
  • Scalar multiplication: (cu) · v = c(u · v)
  • Perpendicular vectors: u · v = 0 if and only if u ⊥ v

Finding the Angle Between Vectors

We can rearrange the dot product formula to find the angle:

cos(θ) = (u · v) / (|u||v|)

Projection

The projection of u onto v is:

projᵥ u = ((u · v) / |v|²) v

This gives the component of u in the direction of v.

Why This Matters

The dot product is used everywhere in physics and engineering to calculate work, find angles, project vectors, and determine if vectors are perpendicular.

📝 Example

Example 1: Computing the Dot Product

Problem: Find u · v for u = ⟨1, 2, 3⟩ and v = ⟨4, -1, 2⟩

Step 1: Multiply corresponding components

u · v = (1)(4) + (2)(-1) + (3)(2)

Step 2: Simplify

u · v = 4 - 2 + 6 = 8

Example 2: Finding the Angle Between Vectors

Problem: Find the angle between u = ⟨1, 0, 0⟩ and v = ⟨0, 1, 0⟩

Step 1: Find the dot product

u · v = (1)(0) + (0)(1) + (0)(0) = 0

Step 2: Find the magnitudes

|u| = √(1² + 0² + 0²) = 1
|v| = √(0² + 1² + 0²) = 1

Step 3: Find cos(θ)

cos(θ) = 0 / (1·1) = 0

Step 4: Find θ

θ = 90°

The vectors are perpendicular!

Example 3: Finding the Projection

Problem: Find the projection of u = ⟨3, 4⟩ onto v = ⟨1, 0⟩

Step 1: Find u · v

u · v = (3)(1) + (4)(0) = 3

Step 2: Find |v|²

|v|² = 1² + 0² = 1

Step 3: Find the projection

projᵥ u = (3/1)⟨1, 0⟩ = ⟨3, 0⟩

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Computing the Dot Product

  1. Multiply corresponding components — u₁v₁, u₂v₂, u₃v₃
  2. Add the products — u₁v₁ + u₂v₂ + u₃v₃
  3. Simplify — Get your final answer

Step-by-Step: Finding the Angle

  1. Find u · v — Use the dot product formula
  2. Find |u| and |v| — Compute the magnitudes
  3. Find cos(θ) — Use cos(θ) = (u · v) / (|u||v|)
  4. Find θ — Take the inverse cosine
