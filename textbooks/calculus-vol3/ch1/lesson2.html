Vectors in Three Dimensions

Chapter 1: Vectors and the Geometry of Space Lesson 2 of 6 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to work with vectors in three-dimensional space, including notation, operations, and applications.

In one sentence: Vectors in 3D have three components and can represent quantities like force, velocity, and position in space.

💡 The Idea

3D Coordinate System

Three-dimensional space uses three axes: x, y, and z. The right-hand rule determines the positive direction.

Right-Hand Rule

Point your fingers in the positive x-direction.

Curl them toward the positive y-direction.

Your thumb points in the positive z-direction.

Vector Notation

A vector in 3D has three components:

v = ⟨v₁, v₂, v₃⟩ = v₁i + v₂j + v₃k

Where i, j, k are the unit vectors in the x, y, z directions.

Vector Operations

Addition

u + v = ⟨u₁+v₁, u₂+v₂, u₃+v₃⟩

Scalar Multiplication

cv = ⟨cv₁, cv₂, cv₃⟩

Magnitude

|v| = √(v₁² + v₂² + v₃²)

Unit Vector

u = v/|v| (points in the same direction as v, but has length 1)

Distance Formula

The distance between points P(x₁, y₁, z₁) and Q(x₂, y₂, z₂) is:

d = √[(x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)² + (z₂-z₁)²]

Midpoint Formula

The midpoint between P and Q is:

M = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2, (z₁+z₂)/2)

Why This Matters

Vectors in 3D are essential for physics, engineering, computer graphics, and many other fields. They allow us to describe and manipulate quantities in three-dimensional space.

📝 Example

Example 1: Vector Operations

Problem: Given u = ⟨1, 2, 3⟩ and v = ⟨4, -1, 2⟩, find u + v, 2u, and |v|

Step 1: Find u + v

u + v = ⟨1+4, 2+(-1), 3+2⟩ = ⟨5, 1, 5⟩

Step 2: Find 2u

2u = ⟨2·1, 2·2, 2·3⟩ = ⟨2, 4, 6⟩

Step 3: Find |v|

|v| = √(4² + (-1)² + 2²) = √(16 + 1 + 4) = √21

Example 2: Finding a Unit Vector

Problem: Find the unit vector in the direction of v = ⟨3, 4, 0⟩

Step 1: Find the magnitude

|v| = √(3² + 4² + 0²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5

Step 2: Divide by the magnitude

u = v/|v| = ⟨3/5, 4/5, 0⟩

Example 3: Distance and Midpoint

Problem: Find the distance and midpoint between P(1, 2, 3) and Q(4, 6, 8)

Step 1: Find the distance

d = √[(4-1)² + (6-2)² + (8-3)²]
= √[9 + 16 + 25] = √50 = 5√2

Step 2: Find the midpoint

M = ((1+4)/2, (2+6)/2, (3+8)/2) = (5/2, 4, 11/2)

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Working with 3D Vectors

  1. Write the vector — Use ⟨v₁, v₂, v₃⟩ notation
  2. Perform operations — Add, subtract, or multiply by scalar
  3. Find magnitude — Use √(v₁² + v₂² + v₃²)
  4. Find unit vector — Divide by magnitude
  5. Interpret — What does the result mean?

Step-by-Step: Distance and Midpoint

  1. Identify the points — P(x₁, y₁, z₁) and Q(x₂, y₂, z₂)
  2. Apply distance formula — d = √[(x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)² + (z₂-z₁)²]
  3. Apply midpoint formula — M = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2, (z₁+z₂)/2)
  4. Simplify — Reduce radicals if possible
