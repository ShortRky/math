Vectors in the Plane

Chapter 1: Vectors and Space Lesson 1 of 6 ⏱️ 30-40 minutes

🎯 Goal

Understand what vectors are, how to represent them, and how to perform basic operations.

In one sentence: A vector is a quantity with both magnitude and direction.

💡 The Idea

What is a Vector?

A vector is a mathematical object that has both:

  • Magnitude (length or size)
  • Direction (which way it points)

Examples: velocity, force, displacement

Representing Vectors

In the plane, a vector can be written as:

v = ⟨a, b⟩ or v = ai + bj

Where:

  • a is the horizontal component (x-component)
  • b is the vertical component (y-component)
  • i = ⟨1, 0⟩ (unit vector in x-direction)
  • j = ⟨0, 1⟩ (unit vector in y-direction)

Vector Operations

Addition

⟨a, b⟩ + ⟨c, d⟩ = ⟨a+c, b+d⟩

Add corresponding components

Scalar Multiplication

k⟨a, b⟩ = ⟨ka, kb⟩

Multiply each component by the scalar

Magnitude

|v| = √(a² + b²)

Distance formula from origin

Why This Matters

Vectors are essential in physics for describing forces, velocities, and accelerations. In mathematics, they're the foundation for multivariable calculus, linear algebra, and more.

📝 Example

Example 1: Given v = ⟨3, 4⟩, find |v|

Step 1: Identify components

a = 3, b = 4

Step 2: Apply magnitude formula

|v| = √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5

Step 3: Conclusion

The vector has magnitude 5.

Example 2: Add v = ⟨1, 2⟩ and w = ⟨3, -1⟩

Step 1: Add components

v + w = ⟨1+3, 2+(-1)⟩ = ⟨4, 1⟩

Step 2: Conclusion

The sum is ⟨4, 1⟩.

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Working with Vectors

  1. Identify the components — What are a and b?
  2. Apply the operation — Addition, scalar multiplication, or magnitude
  3. Simplify — Combine like terms
  4. Check your answer — Does it make sense?
