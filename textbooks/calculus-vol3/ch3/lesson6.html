Applications of Multiple Integrals

Chapter 3: Multiple Integrals Lesson 6 of 6 ⏱️ 45-55 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to apply multiple integrals to calculate physical quantities like mass, center of mass, and moments of inertia.

In one sentence: Multiple integrals allow us to calculate physical properties of objects with varying density and complex shapes.

💡 The Idea

Mass

The mass of a region with density ρ(x, y) is:

M = ∬ᴿ ρ(x, y) dA

For 3D regions:

M = ∭ₑ ρ(x, y, z) dV

Center of Mass

The center of mass (x̄, ȳ) for a 2D region is:

x̄ = (1/M) ∬ᴿ x ρ(x, y) dA
ȳ = (1/M) ∬ᴿ y ρ(x, y) dA

For 3D regions, add z̄ = (1/M) ∭ₑ z ρ(x, y, z) dV

Moments of Inertia

The moment of inertia about the x-axis is:

Iₓ = ∬ᴿ y² ρ(x, y) dA

About the y-axis:

Iᵧ = ∬ᴿ x² ρ(x, y) dA

About the origin:

Iₒ = ∬ᴿ (x² + y²) ρ(x, y) dA

Probability

For a joint probability density function f(x, y):

P((X, Y) ∈ R) = ∬ᴿ f(x, y) dA

Where ∬ f(x, y) dA = 1 over the entire region.

Why This Matters

These applications are fundamental in physics and engineering. Mass and center of mass are essential for structural analysis, moments of inertia for rotational dynamics, and probability for statistics and machine learning.

📝 Example

Example 1: Mass of a Plate

Problem: Find the mass of a rectangular plate 0 ≤ x ≤ 2, 0 ≤ y ≤ 3 with density ρ(x, y) = x + y

Step 1: Set up the integral

M = ∫₀² ∫₀³ (x + y) dy dx

Step 2: Integrate with respect to y

∫₀³ (x + y) dy = [xy + y²/2]₀³ = 3x + 9/2

Step 3: Integrate with respect to x

∫₀² (3x + 9/2) dx = [3x²/2 + 9x/2]₀² = 6 + 9 = 15

The mass is 15 units.

Example 2: Center of Mass

Problem: Find the center of mass of the plate from Example 1

Step 1: Find x̄

x̄ = (1/15) ∫₀² ∫₀³ x(x + y) dy dx
= (1/15) ∫₀² ∫₀³ (x² + xy) dy dx
= (1/15) ∫₀² [x²y + xy²/2]₀³ dx
= (1/15) ∫₀² (3x² + 9x/2) dx
= (1/15) [x³ + 9x²/4]₀²
= (1/15)(8 + 9) = 17/15

Step 2: Find ȳ

ȳ = (1/15) ∫₀² ∫₀³ y(x + y) dy dx
= (1/15) ∫₀² ∫₀³ (xy + y²) dy dx
= (1/15) ∫₀² [xy²/2 + y³/3]₀³ dx
= (1/15) ∫₀² (9x/2 + 9) dx
= (1/15) [9x²/4 + 9x]₀²
= (1/15)(9 + 18) = 27/15 = 9/5

The center of mass is at (17/15, 9/5).

Example 3: Moment of Inertia

Problem: Find the moment of inertia about the x-axis for the plate from Example 1

Step 1: Set up the integral

Iₓ = ∫₀² ∫₀³ y²(x + y) dy dx

Step 2: Integrate with respect to y

∫₀³ y²(x + y) dy = ∫₀³ (xy² + y³) dy
= [xy³/3 + y⁴/4]₀³ = 9x + 81/4

Step 3: Integrate with respect to x

∫₀² (9x + 81/4) dx = [9x²/2 + 81x/4]₀²
= 18 + 81/2 = 117/2

The moment of inertia is 117/2 units.

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Finding Mass

  1. Identify the region — Sketch the shape
  2. Write the density — ρ(x, y) or ρ(x, y, z)
  3. Set up the integral — ∬ ρ dA or ∭ ρ dV
  4. Evaluate — Integrate from inside to outside

Step-by-Step: Finding Center of Mass

  1. Find the mass M — Use the mass integral
  2. Find x̄ — (1/M) ∬ x ρ dA
  3. Find ȳ — (1/M) ∬ y ρ dA
  4. Find z̄ (if 3D) — (1/M) ∭ z ρ dV
