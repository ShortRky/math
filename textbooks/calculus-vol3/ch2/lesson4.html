Motion in Space

Chapter 2: Vector Functions Lesson 4 of 7 ⏱️ 35-45 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to analyze motion in space using position, velocity, and acceleration vectors.

In one sentence: Motion in space is described by position, velocity, and acceleration vectors that are related through differentiation.

💡 The Idea

Position, Velocity, and Acceleration

For a particle moving in space:

Position: r(t) = ⟨x(t), y(t), z(t)⟩
Velocity: v(t) = r'(t) = ⟨x'(t), y'(t), z'(t)⟩
Acceleration: a(t) = v'(t) = r''(t) = ⟨x''(t), y''(t), z''(t)⟩

Relationships

  • Velocity is the derivative of position
  • Acceleration is the derivative of velocity
  • Speed is the magnitude of velocity: |v(t)|

Finding Position from Velocity

If you know velocity and initial position, you can find position:

r(t) = ∫v(t) dt + r₀

Where r₀ is the initial position.

Finding Position from Acceleration

If you know acceleration, initial velocity, and initial position:

v(t) = ∫a(t) dt + v₀
r(t) = ∫v(t) dt + r₀

Projectile Motion

For projectile motion with initial velocity v₀ and angle θ:

x(t) = v₀ cos(θ) · t
y(t) = v₀ sin(θ) · t - (1/2)gt²

Where g is the acceleration due to gravity.

Why This Matters

Understanding motion in space is essential for physics, engineering, and computer graphics. It's the foundation for analyzing everything from projectiles to satellites to animations.

📝 Example

Example 1: Finding Velocity and Acceleration

Problem: A particle has position r(t) = ⟨t², 2t, t³⟩. Find velocity and acceleration.

Step 1: Find velocity

v(t) = r'(t) = ⟨2t, 2, 3t²⟩

Step 2: Find acceleration

a(t) = v'(t) = ⟨2, 0, 6t⟩

Step 3: Find speed at t = 1

v(1) = ⟨2, 2, 3⟩
Speed = |v(1)| = √(4 + 4 + 9) = √17 ≈ 4.12

Example 2: Finding Position from Velocity

Problem: A particle has velocity v(t) = ⟨2t, 3t², 1⟩ and starts at r(0) = ⟨1, 2, 3⟩. Find r(t).

Step 1: Integrate velocity

r(t) = ∫v(t) dt = ⟨t² + C₁, t³ + C₂, t + C₃⟩

Step 2: Use initial condition

r(0) = ⟨C₁, C₂, C₃⟩ = ⟨1, 2, 3⟩
C₁ = 1, C₂ = 2, C₃ = 3

Step 3: Write final answer

r(t) = ⟨t² + 1, t³ + 2, t + 3⟩

Example 3: Projectile Motion

Problem: A ball is thrown with initial velocity 50 m/s at 30°. Find the position at t = 2 seconds (g = 9.8 m/s²).

Step 1: Find initial velocity components

v₀ₓ = 50 cos(30°) = 50 · (√3/2) ≈ 43.3 m/s
v₀ᵧ = 50 sin(30°) = 50 · (1/2) = 25 m/s

Step 2: Write position equations

x(t) = 43.3t
y(t) = 25t - 4.9t²

Step 3: Evaluate at t = 2

x(2) = 43.3 · 2 = 86.6 m
y(2) = 25 · 2 - 4.9 · 4 = 50 - 19.6 = 30.4 m

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Finding Velocity and Acceleration

  1. Start with position — r(t) = ⟨x(t), y(t), z(t)⟩
  2. Differentiate for velocity — v(t) = r'(t)
  3. Differentiate again for acceleration — a(t) = v'(t)
  4. Find speed if needed — |v(t)|
  5. Evaluate at specific times — Plug in t values

Step-by-Step: Finding Position from Velocity

  1. Integrate velocity — r(t) = ∫v(t) dt
  2. Add constants — Include C₁, C₂, C₃
  3. Use initial condition — r(t₀) = given position
  4. Solve for constants — Find C₁, C₂, C₃
  5. Write final answer — r(t) with all constants determined
