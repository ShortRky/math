Motion in Space

Chapter 2: Vector Functions Lesson 4 of 7 ⏱️ 35-45 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to analyze motion in space using position, velocity, and acceleration vectors.

In one sentence: Motion in space is described by position, velocity, and acceleration vectors that are related through differentiation.

💡 The Idea

Position, Velocity, and Acceleration

For a particle moving in space:

Position: r(t) = ⟨x(t), y(t), z(t)⟩
Velocity: v(t) = r'(t) = ⟨x'(t), y'(t), z'(t)⟩
Acceleration: a(t) = v'(t) = r''(t) = ⟨x''(t), y''(t), z''(t)⟩

Speed

The speed is the magnitude of the velocity:

Speed = |v(t)| = √[(x'(t))² + (y'(t))² + (z'(t))²]

Projectile Motion

For a projectile launched with initial velocity v₀ at angle θ:

x(t) = v₀ cos(θ) t
y(t) = v₀ sin(θ) t - (1/2)gt²

Where g is the acceleration due to gravity.

Circular Motion

For uniform circular motion with radius r and angular velocity ω:

r(t) = ⟨r cos(ωt), r sin(ωt)⟩
v(t) = ⟨-rω sin(ωt), rω cos(ωt)⟩
a(t) = ⟨-rω² cos(ωt), -rω² sin(ωt)⟩

Why This Matters

Understanding motion in space is essential for physics, engineering, and computer graphics. It allows us to predict trajectories, design orbits, and simulate realistic motion.

📝 Example

Example 1: Finding Velocity and Acceleration

Problem: A particle has position r(t) = ⟨t², 2t, t³⟩. Find the velocity and acceleration at t = 1.

Step 1: Find the velocity

v(t) = r'(t) = ⟨2t, 2, 3t²⟩

Step 2: Find the acceleration

a(t) = v'(t) = ⟨2, 0, 6t⟩

Step 3: Evaluate at t = 1

v(1) = ⟨2, 2, 3⟩
a(1) = ⟨2, 0, 6⟩

Example 2: Finding Speed

Problem: Find the speed of a particle with velocity v(t) = ⟨3, 4t, 0⟩ at t = 2.

Step 1: Find the magnitude

|v(t)| = √[3² + (4t)² + 0²]
= √[9 + 16t²]

Step 2: Evaluate at t = 2

|v(2)| = √[9 + 16(4)] = √[9 + 64] = √73 ≈ 8.54

Example 3: Projectile Motion

Problem: A ball is thrown with initial velocity 20 m/s at 30°. Find the position at t = 2 seconds (g = 9.8 m/s²).

Step 1: Find initial velocity components

v₀ₓ = 20 cos(30°) = 20 · (√3/2) = 10√3 m/s
v₀ᵧ = 20 sin(30°) = 20 · (1/2) = 10 m/s

Step 2: Write position equations

x(t) = 10√3 t
y(t) = 10t - (1/2)(9.8)t² = 10t - 4.9t²

Step 3: Evaluate at t = 2

x(2) = 10√3 · 2 = 20√3 ≈ 34.64 m
y(2) = 10(2) - 4.9(4) = 20 - 19.6 = 0.4 m

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Analyzing Motion

  1. Find the position function — r(t) = ⟨x(t), y(t), z(t)⟩
  2. Find velocity — v(t) = r'(t)
  3. Find acceleration — a(t) = v'(t)
  4. Find speed — |v(t)|
  5. Evaluate at specific times — Plug in t values

Step-by-Step: Projectile Motion

  1. Find initial velocity components — v₀ₓ and v₀ᵧ
  2. Write position equations — x(t) and y(t)
  3. Find velocity — Differentiate position
  4. Find maximum height — When vᵧ = 0
  5. Find range — When y = 0 again
