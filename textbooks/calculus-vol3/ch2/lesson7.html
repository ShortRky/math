Directional Derivatives and the Gradient

Chapter 2: Vector Functions Lesson 7 of 7 ⏱️ 40-50 minutes

🎯 Goal

Learn how to find the rate of change of a function in any direction and understand the gradient vector.

In one sentence: The directional derivative measures how fast a function changes in a given direction, and the gradient points in the direction of maximum increase.

💡 The Idea

Directional Derivative

The directional derivative of f in the direction of unit vector u is:

Dᵤf = ∇f · u

Where ∇f is the gradient of f.

Example

For f(x, y) = x²y and u = ⟨1/√2, 1/√2⟩:

∇f = ⟨2xy, x²⟩
Dᵤf = ⟨2xy, x²⟩ · ⟨1/√2, 1/√2⟩
= 2xy/√2 + x²/√2 = (2xy + x²)/√2

The Gradient

The gradient of f(x, y) is:

∇f = ⟨∂f/∂x, ∂f/∂y⟩

For f(x, y, z):

∇f = ⟨∂f/∂x, ∂f/∂y, ∂f/∂z⟩

Properties of the Gradient

  • ∇f points in the direction of maximum increase
  • |∇f| is the maximum rate of increase
  • ∇f is perpendicular to level curves
  • ∇f = 0 at critical points

Maximum and Minimum Rates of Change

The maximum rate of change of f at a point is |∇f| in the direction of ∇f.

The minimum rate of change is -|∇f| in the direction of -∇f.

The rate of change is zero in directions perpendicular to ∇f.

Level Curves and Surfaces

Level curves: f(x, y) = c

Level surfaces: f(x, y, z) = c

The gradient is always perpendicular to level curves/surfaces.

Why This Matters

Directional derivatives and gradients are essential for optimization, physics (finding forces), and machine learning (gradient descent). They tell us how functions change in any direction.

📝 Example

Example 1: Finding the Gradient

Problem: Find ∇f for f(x, y) = x²y + xy³

Step 1: Find partial derivatives

∂f/∂x = 2xy + y³
∂f/∂y = x² + 3xy²

Step 2: Write the gradient

∇f = ⟨2xy + y³, x² + 3xy²⟩

Example 2: Finding the Directional Derivative

Problem: Find Dᵤf at (1, 2) for f(x, y) = x²y in the direction u = ⟨3/5, 4/5⟩

Step 1: Find ∇f

∇f = ⟨2xy, x²⟩

Step 2: Evaluate ∇f at (1, 2)

∇f(1, 2) = ⟨2(1)(2), 1²⟩ = ⟨4, 1⟩

Step 3: Compute Dᵤf

Dᵤf = ⟨4, 1⟩ · ⟨3/5, 4/5⟩ = 12/5 + 4/5 = 16/5

Example 3: Finding Maximum Rate of Change

Problem: Find the maximum rate of change of f(x, y) = x²y at (1, 2)

Step 1: Find ∇f at (1, 2)

∇f = ⟨2xy, x²⟩
∇f(1, 2) = ⟨4, 1⟩

Step 2: Find |∇f|

|∇f| = √(4² + 1²) = √17 ≈ 4.12

Step 3: Interpret

The maximum rate of change is √17 in the direction ⟨4, 1⟩.

📋 How to Solve Most Questions

Step-by-Step: Finding the Gradient

  1. Find partial derivatives — Compute ∂f/∂x, ∂f/∂y, etc.
  2. Write as a vector — ∇f = ⟨∂f/∂x, ∂f/∂y, ...⟩
  3. Evaluate at a point — Plug in coordinates if needed
  4. Interpret — Direction of maximum increase

Step-by-Step: Finding Directional Derivatives

  1. Find ∇f — Compute the gradient
  2. Find unit vector u — Normalize the direction vector
  3. Compute dot product — Dᵤf = ∇f · u
  4. Evaluate at point — Plug in coordinates
  5. Interpret — Rate of change in that direction
